Chocolate Pot

Tiffany & Co. · 1879 · Silver, patinated copper, gold, and ivory
The Met · CC0

✦ Read about this

This morning I think of the small mingling of materials that makes a single vessel: silver, patinated copper, gold, and ivory, brought together in 1879 by Tiffany & Co. A chocolate pot, made not just to hold warmth but to be looked at, turned in the light, admired.

There is something quietly generous in the impulse to lavish such craft on an ordinary ritual. Someone decided that a morning cup deserved gold at its edges, deserved the deep tones of patinated copper, deserved care.

I take that as a small instruction for the day. The plain acts — pouring, waiting, sipping — can be honored. Beauty does not wait for grand occasions; it gathers around the everyday, asking only that we notice.

✦ Read about this

Listen to how this poem moves — not in tidy measure but in surge and crash, "crumbling, fore-foundering, thundering all-surfy seas." Hopkins reaches after a sound, a breath, a power he cannot quite name, and the language strains gloriously in the reaching. This is sprung rhythm at work: the line bunching its stresses like waves gathering before they break, refusing to be smoothed.

There is something fitting in knowing this innovator wrote unrecognized in his lifetime, his fame arriving only after death. Here he chases a "heart-song powerful" through bush-browed billows and jaunting trumpets, as though the world itself were straining to speak through him.

Let this morning be an invitation to attend — to the south-westerly wind, to the green underneath the glassy barrel of the wave, to whatever in rank-old nature is quietly trying to tell us something.
